    Update your config.

    V2 uses OAuth authentication. Pick one method below — both do the same thing.

    Add to .mcp.json or ~/.claude.json 
    "searchatlas": {
  "type": "http",
  "url": "https://mcp.searchatlas.com/mcp/"
}
    Run in your terminal 
    claude mcp add searchatlas --transport http "https://mcp.searchatlas.com/mcp/"
    Then authenticate: On your next tool call, Claude will prompt you to open an OAuth link in your browser. Log in with your Search Atlas account and authorize — tools are live immediately.
    Rate limit 30 API calls per minute across all tools. Batch operations (bulk apply, bulk generate) count as a single call. Plan workflows accordingly — heavy sequences may need short pauses between steps.
    JD says
    OAuth tokens refresh automatically — you authenticate once and forget about it. If Claude ever asks you to re-auth, just open the link again. No keys to rotate, no secrets to leak. Your IT guy can finally sleep.

    Before you go, read this.

    JD says
    Claude doesn't remember your MCP tools between sessions unless the config is saved. Always add searchatlas to your project config (.mcp.json in your repo root), not just global settings — that way every session starts with tools loaded.
    JD says
    Be specific with your prompts. "Run an audit" makes Claude guess which client. "Run an OTTO audit on clientdomain.com and show me the top 10 critical issues" gets you results in one shot. The more context, the fewer round trips — and the fewer of those 30 calls/min you burn.
    JD says
    Set up your Brand Vault before writing content. Claude uses it to match your client's voice, tone, and terminology. Without it, you'll get generic copy and spend more time editing than you saved. Think of it as teaching Claude who the client actually is.
    JD says
    Warp is your best friend. It keeps your terminal history, lets you search past commands, and formats output cleanly. If you're still using the default Mac terminal, you're making life harder than it needs to be.
    JD says
    Hitting the rate limit? Use batch tools. otto_generate_bulk_recommendations does in 1 call what 50 individual calls would. Same for gbp_bulk_generate_posts and cg_schedule_article_batch.
    JD says
    Keep your working directory organized. Clone the mastermind repo, keep your skills in a /skills folder, and don't scatter .env files across your desktop. Future you will thank present you. Present you never thanks past you, but that's life.
